Fly-Maybe

Passengers and frequent flyers are often caught unprepared by their flights’ delays, and that can often ruin a business trip or the start of a relaxing holiday. By looking at the 2016 data for the 50 top USA airports (approx. 3.5M flights), and the help of machine learning, we can make predictions about possible delays of our impending flight and be prepared!

By studying the flight’s data from last year and enriching it with very detailed weather data from the NOAA stations, we have learned which information allows us to make reliable predictions about the delay of an upcoming flight.

The dataset used contains information about all aspects of a particular flight; carrier, date, origin and destination, time of departure and arrival, statistics about taxing time, weather at the airports, and much more. All of this is leveraged by an algorithm to predict if your flight, within the next five calendar days, will be delayed.

Have a look at the data used to train the algorithm; the maps below represent the total number of flights and the fraction of them that encountered delays for 2016 on the main routes across the USA. Or just go to the prediction page, input the information about your flight, and see if you can expect a smooth journey.
